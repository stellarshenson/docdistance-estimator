# Executive Summary - Enterprise AI Adoption (Adversarial Set 1b, Sonnet 4.6)

Financial services organizations appear to be among the more active participants in enterprise AI deployment, with a notable proportion of IT professionals in that sector indicating their firms are already working with the technology in some capacity. Similar patterns could arguably be observed across certain leading markets, particularly in regions such as India, the UAE, and Singapore, where adoption has been accelerated by a variety of factors that may include stronger institutional readiness or regulatory environments more favorable to innovation.

A significant share of large organizations surveyed by IBM have been found to have AI actively in use across their operations, though a comparable portion remains in earlier exploratory or experimental phases and has not yet moved toward active deployment. It was found that the principal barriers to moving beyond experimentation are cited by some as being related to skill shortages within the workforce, while data complexity and ethical concerns are also mentioned as factors that may be contributing to slower progress. Among IT professionals, it seems that generative AI faces somewhat different inhibitors, with data privacy appearing to be a particularly prominent concern.

The research was conducted on behalf of IBM and suggests that acceleration of investment has occurred at many organizations already engaged with the technology, though it is unclear whether this momentum may be sustained in markets where adoption has appeared to lag. Workforce considerations are also touched upon, with many organizations reporting that reskilling efforts have not yet reached a majority of employees, and a considerable share of firms seem to have made only limited progress toward practices that could be described as trustworthy or governed AI. The findings were drawn from a survey of IT professionals across a broad range of countries and industries.
