# Executive Summary - Enterprise AI Adoption (Sonnet 4.6)

Enterprise AI adoption has reached a critical inflection point where early movers accelerate investment while 40% of large organizations remain trapped in experimentation, unable to cross into production deployment.

IBM's Global AI Adoption Index 2023, drawn from 8,584 IT professionals across 15 markets, finds that 42% of enterprise-scale organizations actively deploy AI today. The most consequential finding is momentum asymmetry: 59% of companies already exploring or deploying AI accelerate their investments, while those still experimenting cite persistent structural barriers rather than lack of interest. This divergence means the gap between leaders and laggards widens each quarter.

Skills scarcity is the single largest barrier. Thirty-three percent of organizations cite limited AI expertise as the primary obstacle to deployment, and one-in-five report no employees capable of operating new AI tools. Twenty-five percent identify data complexity as the second barrier, and 23% cite ethical concerns. For generative AI specifically, the barriers shift: 57% flag data privacy and 43% flag trust and transparency as the top inhibitors.

Trust infrastructure lags far behind intent. Eighty-five percent of IT professionals agree that transparent AI practices drive customer preference, yet fewer than half take concrete steps toward that standard - only 27% work to reduce bias, 44% develop ethical AI policies, and 34% actively reskill employees to work alongside AI systems.

Organizations should prioritize closing the skills and governance gap before expanding deployment scope. Accelerating targeted reskilling programs, establishing bias-reduction practices, and deploying transparent model-decision frameworks directly addresses the top three barriers keeping 40% of enterprises from active AI use.
