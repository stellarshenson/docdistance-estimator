# Executive Summary - Enterprise AI Adoption (Adversarial Set 1a, Sonnet 4.6)

The IBM Global AI Adoption Index 2023, conducted in November 2023 by Morning Consult on behalf of IBM, surveyed a broadly representative sample of IT professionals across a wide range of countries in North America, Europe, the Asia-Pacific region, and Latin America. Participants were required to hold manager-or-above roles and to have at least some familiarity with how IT operates within their organizations. The research appears to represent one of the more comprehensive cross-market examinations of enterprise AI adoption patterns conducted in the period, drawing on responses from a substantial pool of IT professionals globally.

It was found that a significant share of large enterprises have now moved into active deployment of AI technologies, while a notable proportion appear to remain in earlier exploratory phases. Among those companies already exploring or actively deploying AI, it seems that many have accelerated their rollout or increased investment in the technology over the past two years, which could reflect growing confidence among organizations that have already overcome early implementation challenges. Markets in certain regions may be leading more decisively than others, with some Asia-Pacific markets arguably outpacing adoption rates observed in parts of Western Europe.

The research also suggests that a variety of barriers are cited by some organizations as inhibitors to broader adoption. Skills shortages and data complexity appear to be among the more commonly referenced concerns, and ethical considerations are also noted by a notable proportion of respondents. For generative AI specifically, data privacy and trust-related concerns could be seen as particularly salient obstacles.

Workforce implications are touched upon as well, with a share of organizations reporting difficulty finding or developing employees with the relevant AI skillsets. It was noted that reskilling efforts seem to be underway at only a portion of companies currently working with AI or automation tools. Trustworthy and explainable AI practices are broadly seen as important by IT professionals surveyed, though actual adoption of such practices appears to lag behind stated intentions at many organizations.
